# SWOT, Living Lab Vision 2030, and Closing

**Time:** 16:10-16:45

## Purpose
Turn the concrete workshop experience into team learning, Living Lab Vision 2030 orientation, and follow-up actions.

## Key Question
What did today reveal about our strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and risks when building demonstrators together, and how does this inform the Living Lab Vision 2030?

## Objectives
- Reflect on technical, communication, research, deployment, and operational perspectives.
- Identify reusable practices for future Living Lab demonstrators.
- Locate Scaddy-V2 and related demonstrators within the Living Lab Vision 2030.
- Agree on concrete next steps, owners, and collaboration formats.

## Living Lab Vision 2030
Use the day as concrete evidence for the longer-term vision. The discussion should connect Scaddy-V2, Nanobot-based demonstrators, Digital Living Lab infrastructure, and mixed-team workflows to the question: what should the Living Lab be able to build, host, explain, and transfer by 2030?

Possible anchors:
- Scaddy-V2 as a reusable AI demonstrator pattern, not only a one-day prototype.
- Further public-facing demos for visitors, events, teaching, research communication, and transfer.
- Joint team projects between Leipzig and Dresden.
- Regular meetups or demo days to turn experiments into reusable practice.
- Transfer-area formats for partners, public events, schools, industry, or research showcases.
- Funding, proposals, and follow-up applications that need demonstrator evidence or team alignment.

## SWOT Prompts
- Strengths: What worked because of our team mix?
- Weaknesses: Where did handoffs, tooling, or assumptions slow us down?
- Opportunities: What can become a reusable template, service, or standard?
- Threats: What could make future demonstrators fragile, unsafe, inaccessible, or hard to maintain?

## Perspective Prompts
- Living Lab: What makes this usable for visitors?
- PR/communications: What makes it understandable and accessible?
- HPC/deployment: What makes it operable and secure?
- Developers: What makes it maintainable?
- Researchers: What makes it scientifically useful and honest?
- Everyone: What did you learn about another team's responsibilities?

## Next-Step Questions
- What should happen next with Scaddy-V2?
- Which feature tracks should become follow-up work, and which should stop here?
- Which additional demos would be valuable for the Living Lab or Digital Living Lab?
- Which joint Leipzig-Dresden team projects are realistic after this retreat?
- Which events, meetups, transfer formats, or public showcases could use the result?
- Which funding ideas, proposals, or strategic initiatives could build on today's work?
- What needs an owner before people leave the room?

## Expected Output
- Short SWOT.
- Three to five follow-up actions.
- Named owners for the most important next steps.
- Initial placement of Scaddy-V2 and related demos within the Living Lab Vision 2030.
- Candidate formats for continued collaboration: demos, meetups, joint projects, events, transfer, or proposals.

## Moderation Notes
- Keep the discussion grounded in today's concrete experience; avoid abstract strategy talk only.
- Make sure all perspectives are heard before prioritizing actions.
- Separate immediate next steps from longer-term Vision 2030 ideas.
- Convert broad wishes into named follow-ups where possible.
- End with owners, not only ideas.
