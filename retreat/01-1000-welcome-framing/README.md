# 10:00-10:15 - Welcome, Framing, and Day Objective

## Purpose
Create a shared reason for the day: demonstrators are not just technical artifacts, but tools for science communication, transfer, public engagement, and internal learning.

## Key Question
Why are demonstrators central to our work in science communication, transfer, and the Living Lab?

## Objectives
- Establish that Scaddy-V2 is a learning and demonstrator exercise, not a Scaddy-V1 rebuild.
- Make the different team perspectives explicit from the beginning.
- Define success for the day in terms of working artifact, reusable material, and shared awareness.

## Facilitator Notes
- Emphasize the curve: experience, compare, enable, specify, improve, sprint, deploy, document, pitch, reflect.
- Name the teams in the room: Living Lab, HPC/deployment, web app and AI developers, AI researchers, PR/communications.
- Explain that non-coders have real production roles: prompting, testing, wording, accessibility, documentation, story, and spec keeping.

## Discussion Questions
- What makes a demonstrator successful for visitors?
- What makes it maintainable for the team after the event?
- Where do communication quality, technical reliability, and scientific accuracy collide?
- Which responsibilities are often invisible to other sub-teams?

## Expected Output
- Shared statement of what a successful day looks like.
- Agreement that the workshop is both a build session and a perspective-exchange exercise.
